## The Backlash Scenario

Not everyone celebrates this trajectory. Cricket's romantic traditionalists — and they're influential — argue biomechanics optimization homogenizes technique, removing the sport's aesthetic diversity. **Former England captain Michael Vaughan wrote in The Telegraph (May 2, 2026)** that "we're producing batting robots, not artists."

There's also an equity concern: **biomechanics facilities cost ₹3-8 crore to establish**, creating a two-tier system where wealthy franchises (IPL, Big Bash, The Hundred) pull further ahead of resource-constrained cricket boards in associate nations. This could accelerate cricket's concentration of talent and viewership, undermining the ICC's globalization efforts.

The counterfactual: What if governing bodies mandate "open-source biomechanics" — requiring franchises to share anonymized technique optimization insights with national cricket boards? It's politically challenging but would democratize player development while still allowing competitive differentiation through coaching application.
